This experiment is part of a research study conducted by Dr. Daniel Yurvosky and Dr. Bonnie Nozari at Carnegie Mellon University.

Source of Funding: National Science Foundation, internal CMU funds

Summary
This consent form contains information about the study that you are about to participate in. Your participation allows us to learn about language processing in the human brain. The data from this study might help us design better methods for language learning in children, second-language learning in adults, and re-learning of language in individuals with brain damage.

Purpose
The purpose of the research is to investigate how people express their thoughts using words and sentences, how they detect and correct their errors, and how they learn new words.

Procedures
In this experiment, you will see pictures, words, or video clips, and will respond to them verbally or by pointing or pressing buttons. You may be asked to respond as quickly and as accurately as you can. The procedures will be explained in detail in the instructions for each task. Your participation is voluntary, and you can terminate your participation at any point.

During this activity, we may ask you to:

  • Make decisions about words, sentences or pictures
  • Name pictures or describe visual events
  • Read, write, or type words or sentences
  • Click on objects on the screen or respond by pressing buttons
  • Memorize and recall words, phrases, or pictures you may have seen or heard during the experiment

    Participant Requirements
    You are eligible if:
  • You are 18 years of age or older.
  • You have not been diagnosed with any psychiatric or neurological impairments (such as ADD, anxiety disorders, etc.), and you are not actively using psychotropic medication (with the exception of anti-depressants).
  • __*__ This study requires being a native speaker of English.

    • Risks
    The risks and discomfort associated with participation in this study are no greater than those ordinarily encountered in daily life or during other online activities. You may experience fatigue and frustration in some tasks, but you will be given breaks to alleviate any potential for stress or boredom, and you are free to withdraw your participation at any time.

We would do this without getting additional informed consent from you (or your legally authorized representative).
